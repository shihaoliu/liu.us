<p style="font-size: 1.3rem;"> Hi there 👋👋.  Glad to see you here!! 🎊</p>

I am a graduate student in the [Master of Science in Machine Learning](https://ml.cmu.edu/academics/primary-ms-machine-learning-masters)(MSML) program at [Carnegie Mellon University](https://cmu.edu)(CMU). What excites me most about computer science research is my long-held belief that it is uniquely positioned to enable people to dedicate their time to what matters most to them. Almost everyone encounters tasks they consider necessary yet tedious, whether it's routine paperwork or household chores. Computer science—particularly machine learning (ML), artificial intelligence (AI), and natural language processing(NLP)—has demonstrated remarkable potential to assist with these responsibilities, freeing up time for more meaningful pursuits. Driven by my passion for the field and my conviction in its transformative potential, my goal is to become a research scientist exploring the frontiers of machine learning.

Currently, I am a member of the [NeuLab](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~neulab/) at CMU, where I am advised by Prof. [Graham Neubig](https://www.phontron.com/) and working with [Yueqi Song](https://yueqis.github.io/). My current research focuses on the challenging task of fine-tuning long-context multimodal models for general-purpose agents. Specifically, I am investigating the fundamental challenges of training large language models to process extremely long, multimodal sequences—often exceeding 128K tokens—which combine text, screenshots, and action sequences. My work addresses key questions about the fundamental limits and trade-offs in performance at these extreme context lengths, as well as how different memory management strategies and data selection methods influence the learning process.

This work builds upon my foundational research experience as an undergraduate at the [University of Southern California](https://usc.edu)(USC), where I was a recipient of the [CURVE](https://viterbiundergrad.usc.edu/research/curve/) and [Provost](https://careers.usc.edu/experiences/usc-provosts-undergrad-research-fellowships-2/) Fellowships. During my time there, I pursued two research tracks: evaluating LLM performance in text simplification under the guidance of Prof. [Swabha Swayamdipta](https://swabhs.com/) and [Xinyue Cui](https://x-f-cui.github.io/website/), and implementing reinforcement learning models for multi-agent pathfinding with Prof. [Sven Koenig](https://ics.uci.edu/~svenk/) and [Yimin Tang](https://sites.google.com/view/yimintang). This dual experience has provided me with a strong background in both theoretical and applied machine learning, preparing me for my current and future research endeavors.
